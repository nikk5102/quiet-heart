Dear Diary,

I don’t know why love feels this unfair to me.

Why is it that the people who would do anything just to see me smile are the ones I can’t even look at that way… and the one person I would choose without thinking twice is the one who never chose me? It feels like I’m stuck in a loop I never signed up for.

There are people who see me as something precious… something worth loving deeply. And then there are people who make me feel invisible, replaceable… like my presence or absence doesn’t matter at all.

And somehow… my heart keeps choosing the second kind.

Why?



Today… was one of those days that makes you question yourself.

I went to the office because Papa called me. I thought it was just a normal day. I didn’t know someone had already planned everything around me.

Ritik.

The same Ritik I’ve rejected so many times.

And still… he hasn’t stopped.

Not even a little.


When I saw him, he was standing there casually like nothing was different. But something always feels different with him. Like he’s already thinking ten steps ahead — about me.

I didn’t even realize when he handed me flowers.

White lilies.

My favorite.

I froze for a second.

Because I’ve never told anyone that.

“Tumhe kaise pata?” I asked.

He just shrugged, “Bas pata hai.”

Like it wasn’t a big deal.

But it was.

Because someone noticed something about me… that I never said out loud.

And still… I felt nothing.

Then I saw the handwritten note.


Just simple words… but written like they meant something.

I didn’t react much.

He noticed.

He always notices.

But he didn’t say anything.

—

Papa had told me to go meet his mom. She had her operation recently. I hadn’t visited her, and honestly, I felt a little guilty.

But I was more uncomfortable about going with him.

Because with Ritik… nothing is ever “normal.”

Everything becomes… intense.

—

When we reached, his mom saw me and instantly smiled.

“Aa gayi meri bahu?”

I literally didn’t know where to look.

He looked at me. I looked at him.

Awkward silence.

Then she laughed it off, but I could feel it — she meant it, even if jokingly.

We sat together. She talked to me like I already belonged there.

At one point, she held my hand while talking… and I don’t know why, but that made me even more uncomfortable.

Because I knew… I can’t give them what they expect.

—

Then suddenly she asked,

“Kya hua Piya ke saath? Tum dono baat kyun nahi karte?”

I avoided it.

Said, “Kuch nahi.”

She looked at me like she didn’t believe me.

“Bacchi mat ban. Baat kar usse.”

I didn’t want to.

But I still said, “Theek hai.”

And at that exact moment, I saw Ritik smile.

That annoying, knowing smile.

Like — “I know you won’t.”

He even teased me, “Mumma, aapko lagta hai ye baat karegi? Nikita hai ye.”

His mom looked straight at me, “Main bol rahi hoon. Karegi.”

And I just thought — Great....beta marega tu mujhse aaj jyada pange lerra h....

Then she actually called Piya.

And when she walked in… everything felt weird.

We sat together.

Silence.

Then she said, “I’m sorry, Nikita. Meri galti thi.”

I looked at her.

All I could think was — you broke my trust.

How do I just forget that?

Still, I said, “It’s fine.”

But it wasn’t.

Not even close.

After that, he took me to a café.

He was typing something on his phone.

I leaned slightly and said, “Ahem… girlfriend?”

He gave me that look.

Then turned the phone toward me.

“Do you love me?
If yes, breathe.
If no, backflip.”

For a second I just stared at him.

Then I opened YouTube, showed him a backflip video and said, “See? You got your answer.”

He smirked, “But  you still breathe...”

I rolled my eyes.

He laughed.

I didn’t.

It was already 3 PM and neither of us had eaten.

He kept pushing the plate toward me, “bhookhe rehne se tera exaake nahi khilayega tujhe kha le..”

I rolled my eyes...shut up 

Then we went to the fort.

After so long.

Our place.

We sat at that same spot where the whole city looks tiny and quiet.

There was a moment of silence.

And then he said it.

“How do you always choose the wrong people?”

I didn’t answer.

“I’ve been waiting since 11th,” he continued, “And you know how much i like you... even my gamily loves you ... and still you chooses someone else over me?”

I looked at the city instead of him.

“I don’t know,” I said.

Because that’s the truth.

I really don’t know.

He said something that stayed with me.

“I liked you even when tu bas ek ziddi, ladaku ladki thi.”

No filters.

No expectations.

Just… me.

And still, he chose me.

He said he never had a girlfriend.

Because he loved me....and i know the truth he never had because he still had our photo in his room ...everyone knows how much he likes me ...

and he couldn’t be with someone he doesn’t actually like....and after few minutes 

And I just said casually, “Shaadi toh karni padegi.”

He looked straight at me and said softly,

“Toh tu kar le na. Kya kami hai?”

Like it was that simple.

Like I was enough.

And I said,

“Kami kuch nahi hai....i just don't like you in that way”

And I stood up.

“Chal, chlte h ghar....late ho raha hai.”

I thought we were going home.

But of course…

he is RITIK and he is not a normal guy so as expected He had more plans.

He took me to Raas Café.

I had no idea.

He lied about meeting friends because he knew I wouldn’t come.

When I walked in, I saw the setup.

Candlelight.

Decor.

Everything.

For me.

And something inside me got irritated instead of happy.

“Tu pagal hai? are you out of your mind? Itna kharcha faltu ka? I don’t even consider you a good friend. Main jaa rahi hoon.”

I turned to leave.

Not even a second of hesitation this time. I didn’t want to stay. I didn’t want to be there — not for the dinner, not for the effort, not for him.

This wasn’t something I asked for.
This wasn’t something I felt.
And I didn’t want to pretend.

In the world I know, people are scared of him.

Of his anger.
Of the way he handles things.
He’s not the kind of person people mess with.

He’s… dangerous in his own way.

Even I was scared of him once.

I just always knew — he won’t do anything in front of me.

And maybe that’s why… I never took him seriously.

As I turned, he held my hand.

Lightly.

Just enough to stop me for a second.

Just enough to say — don’t go.

“Please mat jaa… khana hi toh hai. Baith ke kha lete hain.”

His voice wasn’t demanding.

Not like him.

Not like the person everyone knows.

I pulled my hand back.

Still irritated.....Still annoyed.

“Tu pagal hai? Are you out of your mind? Itna kharcha faltu ka? I don’t even consider you a good friend. Main jaa rahi hoon.”

And I meant it.

Every word.

For a second… he didn’t say anything.

And that silence felt strange.

Because he’s never quiet.

Then he spoke.....Slowly.

Like he was thinking before every word.

“I promise… kuch nahi bolunga.”

Pause.

“Na irritate karunga.”

Another pause.

“Bas sath baith kar khana kha le…”

His voice dropped at the end.

Almost like he didn’t have the strength to say more.

I looked at him.

Properly.

For the first time in that moment.

And something felt… off.

He wasn’t standing like himself, without attitude... ego.... or control.

Just… standing there.

Like everything depended on what I do next.

And then I saw it.

In his eyes.

Something I’ve never seen in him before.

Fear.

Not the kind of people show.

But something deeper.

Like he already knew he can’t stop me… and still didn’t want to let me go.

Someone the whole world is scared of…

Standing there quietly.

Almost like he didn’t want to do anything wrong.

Like even one wrong move would make me walk away.

He looked like he was about to cry.

And that didn’t make sense.

Because people like him don’t cry.

They don’t wait.

They don’t ask.

They don’t hold back.

They don’t stop someone from leaving.

They just let them go.

—

But this time, for me, he didn’t.

He didn’t come even closer.

He didn’t try to hold my hand again.

He just stood there.

Holding himself back.

And for a second…

I didn’t leave.

Not because I wanted to stay.

Not because I felt something.

But because for the first time…

He didn’t feel like the strongest person in the room.

He felt… human.

And somehow…

That stopped me.

So I stayed.

We sat across each other.

Candle between us.

He kept looking at me like this moment meant everything to him.

Like this dinner, this time, this small effort… mattered more than anything else.

For me…

it was just another moment I couldn’t feel.

We talked.

About random things.

About life.

About nothing important.

He tried to keep the conversation going.
He smiled a lot.
He laughed at small things.

And I just… sat there.

Listening. Responding. Existing.

But not really *there*.

Because the whole time, there was only one thought in my head—

I wish Mohit was here.

I wish it was him sitting in front of me.
I wish he was the one doing all this.
Or maybe… not even all this.

Maybe just him being there would’ve been enough.

Just his presence.
Just his face across the table.

And everything would’ve felt different.

Everything would’ve felt… real.

But it wasn’t him.

And maybe that’s why…

no matter how much he tried,
no matter how perfect the moment was supposed to be—

I felt nothing.

And now I’m here…

Thinking.

Why am I like this?

Why can’t I feel even a little for someone who loves me this much?

Why does my heart still go back to someone who left me with nothing?

Why do I keep choosing pain over peace?

He has everything.

Looks, respect, family, stability.

And more than that…

He has love for me.

A kind of love that doesn’t change, doesn’t leave, doesn’t give up.

Girls chase him.

He has thousands of options.

And still… he stands where he was years ago.

With me.

Only me.

And the truth I keep running from…

I still love Mohit.

I don’t feel anything for anyone else.

Not even a little.

And I don’t understand why.

Why am I not able to forget him?

Why did I become this kind of girl… the one who doesn’t feel anything anymore?

Or is it not that I don’t like these things…

Is it just that I don’t like them because it’s not him?

I don’t know.

He broke me.

And still… I love him.

I know somewhere deep down, he has moved on.

But my heart hasn’t.

I still remember everything.

That night.

Those moments.

His face.

His smile.

The way he used to look at me.

His childish behavior.

The way he made everything feel so real.

So mine.

And I can’t forget it.

No matter how hard I try.

What’s wrong with me?

I understand that I can’t give chances to someone else like this.

I know it’s not fair.

But then why…

Why does my heart still ache for him?

Why does it still want him?

Even after everything?

Even after he left?

Even after he broke me?

Why am I still here… loving someone who isn’t mine anymore?
